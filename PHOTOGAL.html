Even a student deeply immersed in computer science and technology can set those aside to pursue an unrelated passion. For me, that passion is photography. During my senior year of high school, I joined my school's ambassador program, where one of my responsibilities was managing the social media page. Since then, I’ve used tools like Photoshop, Lightroom, Canva, and PicArt to create social media content and edit photos.

These skills fueled my interest in photography as a hobby, and it wasn’t until the second semester of my freshman year that I officially started as a photographer. I began by capturing anything I could—whether it was the diverse landscapes of the Georgia Tech campus or occasional photoshoots with friends from home. After a semester of practice and dedication, I became the official photographer for GT EESA during my sophomore year. As I continue with my CS (soon-to-be CM) major, I make sure to manage my time effectively so I can keep improving my photography craft.

Pictures are edited by me with Lightroom.
